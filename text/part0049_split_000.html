Socialism: Utopian and Scientific

FRIEDRICH ENGELS

 

Engels’ polemical work Herr Eugen Dühring’s Revolution in Science, better known as Anti-Dühring, came out in 1878. At the request of his friend Paul Lafargue, Engels arranged three general chapters of Anti-Dührung as a pamphlet dealing with the origins of Marxism and summarizing its view of history. Originally published in French in 1880, Socialism: Utopian and Scientific soon appeared in many other languages and became, along with the Communist Manifesto, the most influential popular presentation of the Marxist position in the late nineteenth century. The text given here is that of the authorized English edition of 1892.
