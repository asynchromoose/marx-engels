Against Personality Cults

KARL MARX

 

Marx may have been the person to coin the now commonly used term “personality cult.” Moreover, this passage from his letter of November 10, 1877, to W. Blos was cited by Nikita Khrushchev in his “secret speech” before the closed session of the Soviet Twentieth Party Congress in February, 1956—to support his claim that any cult of personality, such as Stalin’s, was alien to Marxism and to Communism.321
